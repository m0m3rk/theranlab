How to Convert dBm to Watts

dBm is a logarithmic power unit relative to 1 milliwatt (mW). A +10 dBm increase equals 10× more power; +3 dBm ≈ 2× power.

dBm to Watts — Formula
P(W) = 1W × 10^(P(dBm) / 10) / 1000
P(W) = 10^((P(dBm) − 30) / 10)
Subtract 30 from the dBm value to shift the reference from mW to W, divide by 10, then raise 10 to that power.
Example — Convert 43 dBm to watts
P(W) = 10^((43 − 30) / 10)
P(W) = 10^(13 / 10)
P(W) = 10^1.3
P(W) = 19.95 W
Watts to dBm — Formula
P(dBm) = 10 × log₁₀(P(W)) + 30
Take the base-10 logarithm of the power in watts, multiply by 10, then add 30 to convert from dBW to dBm.
Example — Convert 2 watts to dBm
P(dBm) = 10 × log₁₀(2) + 30
P(dBm) = 10 × 0.3010 + 30
P(dBm) = 3.01 + 30
P(dBm) = 33.01 dBm
